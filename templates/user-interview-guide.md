# User Interview Guide — Discovery

**Purpose:** Understand user problems, behaviors, and motivations before building.  
**Duration:** 40–50 minutes · **Format:** 1:1, video or in-person

---

## Before the Interview

- [ ] Define your top 3 hypotheses to test
- [ ] Recruit users who match your target persona (not friends/family)
- [ ] Set up recording (with permission) or bring a note-taker
- [ ] Prepare to listen 80%, talk 20%

**The Mom Test rule:** Ask about their life, not your idea. Never ask "would you use X?" — it tells you nothing.

---

## Opening (5 min)

> "Thanks for making time. I want to be clear — I'm not selling anything. I'm trying to understand how you currently [do X] so we can build something actually useful. There are no wrong answers, and I might ask follow-up questions that seem obvious — that's intentional."

- "What do you do day-to-day?" *(context setting)*
- "How often do you [relevant behavior]?"

---

## Core Discovery (25 min)

### Understanding Current Behavior

- "Walk me through the last time you [did X]. What triggered it? What did you do?"
- "What tools or workarounds do you use to deal with [problem area]?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of [current process]?"

### Probing the Problem

- "How often does [frustration] happen?"
- "What have you tried to fix it?"
- "What happened the last time it went wrong?"

### Understanding Value

- "What would have to be true for [problem] to no longer be a problem?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [area], what would it be?"

### Budget / Priority Signal (if relevant)

- "Have you ever paid for something to solve this? What was it?"
- "How much time does this cost you per week?"

---

## Testing Hypotheses (10 min)

*Ask about your assumptions indirectly — never pitch.*

- "I've heard some people [do X] to solve this — is that something you've tried?"
- "Some products in this space do [Y]. What's your reaction to that approach?"

---

## Closing (5 min)

- "Is there anything I didn't ask that you think I should know?"
- "Who else do you think I should talk to?"
- "Would it be okay to follow up if I have more questions?"

---

## After the Interview

Within 24 hours:
1. Write up raw notes — what they said, not your interpretation
2. Tag key quotes by theme (pain, behavior, workaround, motivation)
3. Note surprises — things that contradicted your assumptions

After 5+ interviews:
- Look for patterns, not outliers
- Update your problem statement if the evidence points elsewhere
- Identify the riskiest remaining assumption

---

## What to Avoid

| Don't | Do Instead |
|---|---|
| "Would you use this?" | "What do you currently use?" |
| "What features do you want?" | "What's the most painful part of X?" |
| Leading questions ("Don't you think...") | Open questions ("Tell me about...") |
| Pitching your solution | Listening to their problem |
| Interviewing only happy users | Include churned or non-users |
